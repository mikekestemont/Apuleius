When you get a tan, what is actually happening is that the  melanocytes  are producing  melanin pigment  in reaction to ultraviolet light in sunlight.  Ultraviolet light stimulates melanin production.  The pigment has the effect of  absorbing the UV radiation in sunlight, so it protects the cells from UV damage.   Melanin production takes a fair amount of time - that is why most people cannot get a tan in one day. You have to expose yourself to UV for a short period of time to activate the melanocytes. They produce melanin over the course of hours. By repeating this process over 5 to 7 days pigment builds up in your cells to a level that is protective. The previous paragraph applies to Caucasians.
